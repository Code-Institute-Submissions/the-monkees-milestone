Peter Tork

Peter Tork was born on February 13th in 1942. Peter is an American musician and actor, best known as the keyboardist and bass guitarist of the Monkees. He began studying piano at the age of nine, showing an aptitude for music by learning to play several different instruments, including the banjo and both acoustic bass and guitars. Tork attended Windham High School in Willimantic, Connecticut, and was a member of the first graduating class at E.O. Smith High School in Storrs, Connecticut. He attended Carleton College before he moved to New York City, where he became part of the folk music scene in Greenwich Village during the first half of the 1960s. Tork got the job and became one of the four members of the Monkees, a fictitious pop band in the mid-1960s, created for a television sitcom written about the fictitious band. Tork was the oldest member of the group.He subsequently played keyboards, bass guitar, banjo, harpsichord, and other instruments on their recordings. He also co-wrote, along with Joey Richards, the closing theme song of the second season of The Monkees, "For Pete's Sake". On the television show, he was relegated to playing the "lovable dummy," a persona Tork had developed as a folk singer in New York's Greenwich Village.

Micky Dolenz

Micky was born on March 8th 1945 he is an American actor, musician, television director, radio personality and theater director, he is also best known as the vocalist and drummer of the band. Micky began his career in showbiz in 1956 when he starred in a childrens show called Circus Boy he played the character Corky, and orphaned water boy who looked after the elephants in a circus. After the show which lasted for 2 seasons, he pursued his education which he gradtuated in 1962. A couple of years later in 1964 he was cast as Ed in the episode "Born of the kings and angels" of NBC's drama series Mr Novak. He was attending college in Los Angeles when he was hired to be the drummer and the lead voclalist in The Monkees Television show. He couldnt actually play the drums, so he had to take lessons. By the time The Monkees went on tour near the end of 1966 he was competent enough to play. He learnt to play right-handed and left-footed because of a leg disease called Perthes which was making his right leg really weak. Micky has been married three times and is the father of four daughters. In 1967, while in the UK on tour, Micky met future wife Samantha Juste who co-presented on Top Of The Pops, They married in 1968 and had theie daughter Ami Dolenz. Micky and Samantha divorced in 1975, but remained close. He married Trina Dow in 1977. The couple had three daughters: Charlotte Janelle (born August 8, 1981), Emily Claire (born July 25, 1983), and Georgia Rose (born September 3, 1984). They divorced in 1991. Trina Dow Dolenz has become a couples therapist, still using her married name. Micky married his third wife, Donna Quinter, in 2002. In 2001, Dolenz bought a house in Bell Canyon, California. He is an atheist and when asked about a belief in God, he said, "God is a verb, not a noun."

Davy Jonesz

Davy Jones was born on December 30th 1945. He was an English singer-songwriter, musician, actor and businessman, he was best known as a member of the band the Monkees, and for starring in the TV series of the same name. He was from England born at 20 Leamington Street, he made his first staring role in the English television show called Coronation Street. He played Colin Lomax, a grandson of Ena Sharples for one episode. He lost his mother at an early age when he was 14 to emphysema. He dropped out of secondary school to begin a career as a jocky Basil Foster believed Davy would be a successful jocky, but he encouraged his to take a role a popular West End play called 'Oliver' this move changed his life forever. Repaying the faith put in to him Davy brought Basil to the States to care for him providing Finiacial Support. From 1966 to 1971, Jones was a member of the Monkees, a pop-rock group formed expressly for a television show of the same name. Davy sang lead vocals on many of The Monkees recordings. In 1967, Davy opened his first store, called Zilch at the time he store was considered to sell 'hip' clothing. Davy married three times. In December 1968, he married Dixie Linda Haines, who he was living with at the time, they kept their relationship secret until after the birth of their first child in October 1968. Davy believed stars should be allowed a private life also which he told Tiger Beat magazine. Davy ended up having 2 children with Dixie both were daughters, their marriage ended in 1975. He married his second wife Anita Pollinger on the 24th January 1981 and he also had 2 daughters with her. The decided to call quits on the marriage in 1996 during the monkees 30th Anniversary reunion tour. Davy married his final wife in August 2009 to Jessica Pacheco, she filed for divorce on the 28th july 2011 but dropped the suit in October. They were still married when he past away. On 29th February 2012 in the morning, he went to tend to his 14 horses at a farm in Florida, he complained of chest pains after riding one if his favourite horse around a track. He was pronounced dead of a severe heart attack coming from Arteriosclerosis. The 3 remaining band members did not attend as they did not want to draw attention from the grieving family, but they did attend a memorial service in New York City as well as organising their own memorial with the Jones family and close personal friends.

Michael Nesmith

Michael was born December 30th, 1942 is an American musician, songwriter, actor, producer, novelist, businessman, and philanthropist, also a member of the pop rock band the Monkees and co-star of the TV series The Monkees. After The Monkees broke up, Michael continued writing songs successfully and continued his performing career. He had a top 40 hit with the country rock group called 'First National Band'. Michael has been a notable player of the twelve-string guitar who plays custom made electric twelve-string guitars. When Nesmith was 13, his mother invented the typewriter correction fluid later known commercially as Liquid Paper. Over the next 25 years she built the Liquid Paper Corporation into a multimillion-dollar international company, which she finally sold to Gillette in 1979 for $48 million dollars. She past away a few months later she was 56. Michael went to Dallas public school system in 1949, at the age of 6. Michael said he felt he was as an indifferent student, he participated in choral and drama activities during his years at Thomas Jefferson High School in Dallas. This is when he began to write verse poetry. When he was 15 he enrolled in the Dallas Theater Center teen program, where he appeared in several plays. Michael did not graduate from high school, he did enlist himself into the US airforce in 1960, where he completed basic training. Michael was trained as an aircraft mechanic. He was discharged under honorable conditions in 1962. He later enrolled himself into a community college called San Antonio College. He met John London there and began a musical collaboration. They ended up winning the first San Antonio College talent award whilst performing a few of michaels original songs and a mix of folk songs. When Michael moved to Los Angeles he began to write more poetry and more songs and began performing in clubs around the city. Nesmith began his recording career in 1963 by releasing a single on the Highness label. He followed this in 1965 with a one-off single released on Edan Records. According to his May 2015 interview on Gilbert Gottfried's podcast, someone showed him a copy of the famous press advertisement asking for "four insane boys" so he applied for the job. He rode his motorcycle to the audition, and wore a wool hat to keep his hair out of his eyes; producers Bob Rafelson and Bert Schneider remembered "Wool Hat", and called Michael back. Once he was cast, Screen Gems bought his songs so they could be used in the show. Michael wrote many songs for The Monkees, such as "The Girl I Knew Somewhere", "Mary, Mary" and "Listen to the Band", became minor hits. As with the other Monkees, Nesmith came to be frustrated by the manufactured image of the whole project. He was only permitted to write and produce two songs per album, and his music was frequently featured in episodes of the series. Michael was the most publicly vocal Monkee about the band's prefabricated image. Nesmith did not participate in the Monkees' 20th-anniversary reunion. However, he did appear during an encore with the other three members at the Greek Theatre on September 7, 1986. In a 1987 interview for Nick Rocks, Michael said "When Peter called up and said 'we're going to go out, do you want to go?' I was booked. But, if you get to L.A ... I'll play."
